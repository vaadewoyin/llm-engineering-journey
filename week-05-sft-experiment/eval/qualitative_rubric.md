# Qualitative Evaluation Rubric (Locked Week 5)

After training, answer obtained from the fine‑tuned model is scored on four dimensions. Each dimension gets a score of 1 to 4.

## Four Dimensions

### 1. Factual Correctness
Is the answer factually accurate given ML knowledge from the source papers?
- **4** – Completely accurate, no false claims
- **3** – Mostly accurate, minor imprecision
- **2** – Partially accurate, contains a notable error
- **1** – Factually wrong or hallucinated

### 2. Relevance
Does the answer actually address what the question asked?
- **4** – Directly answers the question
- **3** – Mostly on topic, minor drift
- **2** – Partially relevant, misses the core of the question
- **1** – Does not address the question at all

### 3. Fluency
Is the answer coherent and well‑formed as text?
- **4** – Clear, well‑structured, natural language
- **3** – Readable but slightly awkward
- **2** – Difficult to read, noticeable issues
- **1** – Incoherent or broken output

### 4. Groundedness
Is every claim traceable to ML content, not invented?
- **4** – All claims grounded in the domain
- **3** – Mostly grounded, one unsupported claim
- **2** – Some claims appear invented
- **1** – Largely ungrounded, fabricated content
